# Resources: Foundations

## Textbooks

- **Gilbert Strang, *Linear Algebra and Its Applications*** --- The classic reference for applied linear algebra
- **Sheldon Axler, *Linear Algebra Done Right*** --- A more abstract, proof-oriented treatment (great for those with math background)
- **David Lay, *Linear Algebra and Its Applications*** --- Accessible undergraduate text

## Online Courses and Lectures

- [MIT OCW 18.06SC: Linear Algebra (Strang)](https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/18-06sc-linear-algebra-fall-2011/) --- Full video lectures, problem sets, and exams. Covers all foundational topics.
- [3Blue1Brown: Essence of Linear Algebra](https://www.3blue1brown.com/topics/linear-algebra) --- Outstanding visual intuition for vectors, transformations, change of basis, eigenvalues, and more. Highly recommended as a companion to this module.

## Online Notes

- [Matrix Methods in Data Analysis (jokerdii)](https://jokerdii.github.io/matrix-methods-notes/) --- Concise lecture notes covering matrix methods with applications
- [MIT OCW 18.06SC Unit I Summary](https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/18-06sc-linear-algebra-fall-2011/) --- Problem sets and summaries for the foundational unit

## Interactive Tools

- [Immersive Linear Algebra](http://immersivemath.com/ila/index.html) --- Interactive online textbook with embedded visualizations
- [Seeing Theory: Linear Algebra](https://seeing-theory.brown.edu/) --- Visual explorations of mathematical concepts
- [GeoGebra Matrix Calculator](https://www.geogebra.org/m/rMWBHBPx) --- Interactive matrix operations and transformations

## Tensors

- Kolda & Bader (2009). [Tensor Decompositions and Applications](https://doi.org/10.1137/07070111X). SIAM Review. --- The standard reference for tensor methods.
